A storied beginning

Tradition, remade for discovery.

Originally launched in 1907 in Essex, Massachusetts, the Rex Maris was restored from the keel up to unite classic seamanship with a modern ocean-research platform.

A life at sea

Built for strength and endurance, she spent decades working the North Atlantic before beginning a new chapter in science.

Reimagined for research

Every major system was rebuilt or upgraded for safety, reliability, environmental stewardship, and scientific work.

Built for a purpose

Her decks and laboratories support coastal and deep-ocean research, instrument deployment, and collaborative fieldwork.

More than a vessel

A place where craft, curiosity, and community meet — designed to make ambitious work possible at sea.

Our Crew

Fourteen people.
One purpose.

The Rex Maris is only as strong as the people who sail her: mariners, scientists, engineers, and problem-solvers united by a deep respect for the ocean.
